Keeping track of your work is an important and crucial skill. Without a record, there's nothing to look back and reflect upon, and no way to learn from any possible mistakes. As a writer I am always learning and improving upon my skills, even without consciously recognising this; when I look back on records of old works and planning processes, though, it's evident to see the changes. 
I can tend to have trouble remembering things. Keeping written records helps with this. If I don't write down an idea, or the direction I want to take something, or what I'm aiming to convey, I very well might just forget all about it.
I like to keep physical records in notebooks. Something about putting pen to paper makes everything feel more concise and real, even if I do most of my actual writing on my laptop. 
There's something very satisfying on looking back on old drafts and notes. Evidence of the process.
